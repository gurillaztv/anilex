2.Description of the app

ANILEX is a collecting game with which you can discover real animals in your surroundings. You photograph an animal, AI-assisted species recognition suggests a species, and you collect your finds as photo stickers in a personal index. The app also offers game elements such as species profiles, quiz questions, achievements and a personal map of your finds.

The app is free to play, but not ad-free. It is funded by advertising (section 3) and by optional paid in-app purchases (section 4). Without a purchase you can currently catch up to 20 animals per week (the week starts on Monday); you can raise that allowance by choosing to watch rewarded video ads. The stated limits are part of the game design and may change as described in section 13.

8.Respect for wildlife and nature

ANILEX is about observing, not touching. Please treat animals and nature with respect:

  • Keep a sufficient distance and do not disturb animals.
  • Do not lure or feed animals in order to get a photo.
  • Avoid nests, broods, young animals and resting places.
  • Stay on the paths and respect protected areas.
  • Observe the applicable nature conservation, species protection and animal welfare laws as well as local regulations.

9.AI species recognition: an aid only, no warranty

The AI-assisted species recognition only provides a suggestion or estimate of the animal species. It may be incorrect. We accept no warranty for the accuracy, completeness or timeliness of the recognition results and the information displayed.

The species recognition is no substitute for identification by an expert. In particular, do not rely on the app for safety-critical decisions. The app gives no recommendation regarding consumption of animals, plants or fungi. Individual profiles and quick facts do mention general characteristics of a species – including notes on toxicity or defensiveness. Such notes are written for entertainment and are not reliable safety information: they describe the species in general, not the individual animal in front of you, and the recognition itself can be wrong. Never touch, consume or handle a living being in reliance on information from the app – and never treat an animal as harmless simply because the app does not say otherwise.
